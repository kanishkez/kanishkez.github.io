Bidirectional RNNs

Bidirectional RNNS are traditional RNNS with two passes: forward and backward. So there are two sets of hidden states for every timestep (forward and backward). This is so that the context is equally about both the start of the sentence/sequence and the end For example: Kanishk started by reading Metamorphosis, he then read crime and punishment. What book did he read first? In such a question where the model probably learns to connect the word 'read' with books, it might answer crime and punishment instead of Metamorphosis So by using two hidden states where one goes from the start of the sequence to the left and the other from right to left we can sort of solve this problem.

Forward and Backward States

Suppor we take the example of 'I love pizza' The forward state is the same as a regular RNN where it takes 3 tokens at 3 seperate time steps

But in the backward pass it starts from 'pizza' at time step 3 and goes to 'I' at time step 1

After both the passes are done, the two hidden states at timestep 1 with both forward and backward pass are combined to produce an output.

Forwad pass:

Hidden state: $$\overrightarrow{\mathbf{h}_t} = \tanh(\mathbf{W}^{(f)}_h \mathbf{x}_t + \mathbf{U}^{(f)} \overrightarrow{\mathbf{h}({t-1}}) + \mathbf{b}^{(f)}_h)$$

Output: $$y^t=W_{y}h^t+b_{y}$$ here the previous hidden state is used obviously

Backward pass:

Hidden state

$$\overleftarrow{\mathbf{h}_t} = \tanh(\mathbf{W}^{(b)}_h \mathbf{x}_t + \mathbf{U}^{(b)} \overleftarrow{\mathbf{h}({t+1}} )+ \mathbf{b}^{(b)}_h)$$The forward hidden state that was calculated before itself, is used here.

Output: $$y^t=W_{y}h^t+b_{y}$$ An important thing about the backward pass is that the tokens are sent reversed. So 'kanishk is a retard' would become 'retard a is kanishk'

Combining the hidden states:

Normally we concantenate both the states/outputs in a bidirectional rnn So we're just stacking both of the context vectors into a single bigger context vector Forward pass contains information from the past Backward pass contains information from the future So concatinating these two, the model can learn how to use both directions through different weights of course (since both the passes have completely different weights)

Context:

$$h_{t} = \vec{h}_t^{\rightarrow} \,\|\, \vec{h}_t^{\leftarrow}$$
  • Knows what came before and what came after
  • Example: in the sentence “He went to the store”, the word “went” can be better understood with future context (“to the store”). This full context enables the model to disambiguate meanings, handle long dependencies, and improve performance in translation, speech, etc.

Only Encoder is bidirectional the decoder is unidirectional

You can't use future words to regenerate the words you have generated so during inference decoder generates tokens on eat a time from left to right

At each timestep:

Both the forward and backward hidden states are used as both are simultaneously calculated and just concatenated at every timestep theres no sense of direction or the fact that theyre concatenated at a specific timestep. Nothing like that, they are just concatenated.
